The Pomodoro Technique

The Pomodoro Technique is a time-management method built around short, focused work sessions followed by short breaks. A common cycle is 25 minutes of focused work, then a 5 minute break. After four focus sessions, take a longer break.

Why people use it

  • Reduces procrastination by lowering the barrier to start.
  • Helps maintain concentration with clear start/stop boundaries.
  • Builds sustainable work rhythm through regular breaks.

How to use it well

  • Pick one concrete task before starting a focus session.
  • Avoid multitasking during the timer.
  • Use breaks to stand up, stretch, or step away from the screen.
